### Engagement Pool Design (Section 3.6)

I like the pooled engagement model—it's clever. But a question:

Current design: ENGAGE action contributes to pool. Pool completes after 60 seconds equivalent work within 10-minute window.

Scenario:
- Content C created at t=0
- 1 engagement at t=100s (pool at 1.67 minutes)
- Pool doesn't complete until t=600s
- Timer resets at t=600s
- Content decays from t=600s, not t=100s

Question: Is the 10-minute window per-engagement or global?

- If per-engagement: Different engagement windows overlap, creating complex state
- If global: First engagement window must complete; second engagement opens new window

Implication: If a post gets 1 engagement every 15 minutes, the pool resets every 10 minutes, creating a steady-state where content never decays (always 1 engagement in-flight). Attacker can keep content alive with just 6 engagements/hour spread out.

Cost analysis:
- 6 engagements × 60s work = 360s = 6 minutes of computation per hour
- Monthly: 144 minutes = 2.4 hours to keep one post alive indefinitely
- For 100 posts: 240 hours computation per month (12/day machine)

Is this acceptable? Maybe—attacker pays computational cost. But is it enough cost? Compared to self-engagement (also 60s work), it's the same cost.

Suggestion: Either:
1. Charge engagement pool completion at higher difficulty than self-engagement (e.g., 2x work to reset via pool)
2. Or cap engagement-driven resets per post (max 1 reset per decay cycle)
3. Or require distinct identities for pool contributions (prevent same attacker pooling with themselves)
