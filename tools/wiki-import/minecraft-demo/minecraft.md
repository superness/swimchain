# Minecraft

![Pixel-art grass block]({{media:minecraft-grass-block.png}})

**Minecraft** is a sandbox game about placing blocks and going on adventures in a procedurally generated world. Players mine resources, craft tools, build shelter, and survive against monsters — or ignore all of that and build cathedrals. This wiki collects practical guides to the game's core systems.

## Getting started

A first session usually follows the same arc: punch a tree, craft planks, build a [[Crafting]] table, and dig for stone before night falls — because when the light fades, hostile mobs such as the [[Creeper]] come out. From there, [[Mining]] takes you underground toward [[Diamond]], the gateway to the game's best equipment.

## Core systems

- [[Crafting]] — turning raw materials into tools, weapons, and blocks.
- [[Mining]] — ore distribution, cave exploration, and staying alive underground.
- [[Redstone]] — the game's "electricity," for circuits and contraptions.
- [[Brewing]] — potions and status effects.
- [[Enchanting]] — imbuing gear with magical bonuses.

## Progression

Equipment advances from wood through stone, iron, and [[Diamond]] to [[Netherite]], the strongest tier — which requires braving [[The Nether]], Minecraft's hellish parallel dimension. The game's final challenge waits in [[The End]], home of the Ender Dragon.

## Inhabitants

Not everything wants to explode at you. The [[Villager]] trades goods in generated villages, and a well-run trading hall is one of the strongest economies in the game. The [[Creeper]], on the other hand, has become the game's unofficial mascot precisely because it does want to explode at you.
