# The Nether

![Pixel-art Nether portal]({{media:the-nether.png}})

**The Nether** is a dangerous alternate dimension in [[Minecraft]], resembling a vast underground hellscape of fire, lava, and hostile creatures. It is reached through a portal and is essential for late-game progression, since several key materials exist nowhere else.

## Reaching the Nether

A player builds a **Nether portal** from obsidian — typically a rectangular frame at least four blocks wide and five tall — and activates it with flint and steel. Stepping through after a short delay transports the player to the Nether. Distances in the Nether are compressed: one block traveled there equals eight blocks in the normal Overworld, so players often build portal networks to travel long distances quickly.

## Environment and dangers

The Nether has no water (it evaporates instantly) and no natural day-night cycle — it is always dimly lit and full of open lava. Falling into lava, being knocked off ledges by a mob, or wandering into a wall of fire are constant threats. Beds explode if a player tries to sleep, making the Nether one of the few places where a bed becomes a weapon.

The dimension is divided into biomes, including crimson and warped forests, soul sand valleys, and basalt deltas, each with distinct terrain and mobs.

## Mobs and resources

- **Ghast** — a large floating mob that shoots explosive fireballs, which can be deflected back to kill it.
- **Piglin** — a humanoid that will attack players not wearing gold, but can be bartered with for various items.
- **Blaze** — found in Nether fortresses; drops blaze rods, an ingredient needed to reach [[Minecraft]]'s final dimension, the End.

The Nether is also the only source of nether wart (for brewing potions) and ancient debris (refined into netherite, the strongest tool and armor material). Hostile mobs like the [[Creeper]] still spawn in its dark corners, so the same lessons from the surface apply.
