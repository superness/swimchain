# Creeper

The **Creeper** is one of the most recognizable hostile mobs in [[Minecraft]]. It is a green, four-legged creature with a distinctive frowning face, and it attacks by approaching a player in silence and then exploding, destroying nearby blocks and damaging anything close to the blast.

![Pixel-art creeper face]({{media:creeper-face.png}})

## Behavior

Creepers spawn in dark areas with a light level of zero, both on the surface at night and in caves. Unlike most hostile mobs, a Creeper does **not** burn in daylight, so creepers that spawn overnight often survive into the morning and wander the surface.

A Creeper moves toward the nearest player within range. When it comes close, it begins a short fuse — swelling, flashing white, and hissing — before detonating. Backing away during the fuse can cancel the explosion. The blast damages the player, destroys blocks, and can be fatal in the early game before armor is available.

## Charged creepers

If a Creeper is struck by lightning, it becomes a **charged creeper**, surrounded by a blue electrical aura. A charged creeper's explosion is significantly more powerful. When a charged creeper kills a skeleton, zombie, or another creeper, that mob drops its head — the only way to obtain mob heads in survival play.

## Dealing with creepers

- **Bow and arrow** lets you kill a Creeper from range before it can reach you and detonate.
- A **melee hit followed by immediate retreat** can knock the Creeper back and interrupt its fuse.
- **Cats and ocelots** frighten creepers, which flee from them — keeping a cat nearby is a practical defense for a base.

See also: [[Redstone]] for building creeper-proof automatic doors, and [[The Nether]] for a dimension where creepers still spawn in the dark.
