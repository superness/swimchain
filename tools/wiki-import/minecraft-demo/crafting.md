# Crafting

![Pixel-art crafting table]({{media:crafting.png}})

**Crafting** is the primary way players create tools, weapons, armor, and blocks in [[Minecraft]]. Nearly every item in the game is made by arranging materials in a specific pattern, and learning these patterns — called recipes — is central to progressing from bare hands to full iron or diamond gear.

## The crafting grid

Basic crafting uses a 2×2 grid available in the player's inventory at all times, enough for simple recipes like planks, sticks, and torches. To unlock the full set of recipes, the player crafts a **crafting table**, which provides a 3×3 grid. The larger grid is required for most tools, armor, and complex blocks.

## How recipes work

A recipe is defined by the **shape and position** of ingredients on the grid. A pickaxe, for example, needs three of a material across the top row and two sticks down the middle column. Some recipes are **shapeless** — the ingredients can go anywhere, as with mixing dyes or crafting mushroom stew.

Tools come in material tiers, each better than the last: wood, stone, iron, gold, and diamond, with netherite as the strongest upgrade. Higher tiers break blocks faster, last longer, and — for weapons — deal more damage.

## Beyond the table

Several specialized crafting stations extend the system:

- **Furnace** — smelts ores into ingots and cooks food.
- **Anvil** — repairs and renames items and applies enchantments from books.
- **Brewing stand** — makes potions.
- **Smithing table** — upgrades diamond gear to netherite.

Crafting is deeply tied to the rest of the game: gathering materials by mining, farming, and defeating mobs like the [[Creeper]] all feed back into what a player can build, including the components used in [[Redstone]] machines.
