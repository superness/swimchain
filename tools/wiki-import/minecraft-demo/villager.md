# Villager

![Pixel-art villager]({{media:villager.png}})

**Villagers** are peaceful, humanlike inhabitants of [[Minecraft]] who live in naturally generated villages and trade goods with players. They turn a solitary survival game into something closer to a small economy, and a well-managed village becomes one of the most powerful resources a player can build.

## Trading

Each villager takes on a **profession** — such as farmer, librarian, cleric, toolsmith, or armorer — determined by a nearby job-site block like a lectern or blast furnace. Villagers offer trades in their profession, usually exchanging **emeralds** for useful items or accepting goods in return. As a player trades with a villager, it levels up and unlocks better offers, eventually reaching Master tier.

Librarians are especially prized: they trade enchanted books, making them a reliable route into the [[Enchanting]] system without relying on the table's randomness.

## Building a village economy

Players often gather villagers, assign them job blocks, and set up trading halls. A cleric buys rotten flesh and sells useful potions ingredients; a farmer buys crops and sells food and emeralds; a toolsmith can sell enchanted [[Diamond]] gear. Because trades restock when a villager works at its station, a good hall provides a renewable supply of otherwise rare items.

## Defense and growth

Villages attract danger. Zombies hunt villagers at night and can convert them, and raids can threaten the whole settlement, so players wall off villages, add lighting to stop mob spawns, and keep an **Iron Golem** nearby as a guardian. Villagers who have enough food and beds will breed, letting a village grow over time.

Curing a zombie villager — a delicate process using a splash potion and a golden apple — not only saves the villager but permanently lowers its prices, a favorite trick of players chasing the best trades.

See also: [[Enchanting]] for the books librarians sell, and [[Crafting]] for the job-site blocks that assign professions.
