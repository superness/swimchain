# Redstone

**Redstone** is the system in [[Minecraft]] that lets players build working machines, circuits, and contraptions. It is often described as the game's "electricity," and it turns Minecraft from a building game into a platform where players construct logic gates, automatic farms, hidden doors, and even functioning calculators.

## Redstone dust

The core component is **redstone dust**, obtained by mining redstone ore deep underground. Placed on the ground, redstone dust forms wires that carry a signal. A signal has a strength from 0 to 15 and weakens by one level for every block it travels, fading to nothing after 15 blocks unless boosted by a repeater.

## Power sources and components

Signals begin at a **power source** — a lever, button, pressure plate, or redstone torch — and travel through dust to a **component** that does something:

- **Redstone torch** — provides constant power and can act as an inverter (NOT gate).
- **Repeater** — extends a signal back to full strength and adds a short, adjustable delay.
- **Comparator** — compares signals and can read the contents of containers.
- **Piston** — pushes blocks when powered; a sticky piston can also pull them back.

Combining these lets a player build the logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, XOR) that underpin every digital circuit, which is why redstone is frequently compared to real-world [[electrical circuits]].

## Common builds

Beginners often start with an automatic door, a hidden staircase, or a self-harvesting crop farm. Advanced players have built clocks, memory cells, seven-segment displays, and machines that took months to design. Because redstone follows consistent rules, a working contraption in one world behaves identically in another.
