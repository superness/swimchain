# Diamond

![Pixel-art diamond]({{media:diamond.png}})

**Diamond** is one of the most valuable materials in [[Minecraft]], prized for making durable tools, strong armor, and several essential crafting stations. For much of the game's history, diamond gear was the best a player could obtain, and it remains the gateway to the strongest tier, [[Netherite]].

## Finding diamonds

Diamond ore spawns deep underground, most commonly in the lower layers of the world near bedrock. Players reach it by digging down and exploring caves, ravines, and abandoned mine shafts — see [[Mining]] for technique and safety. A key rule: diamond ore can only be harvested with an **iron pickaxe or better**. Mining it with a stone or wooden pickaxe destroys the block and yields nothing, a mistake that has cost countless new players their first find.

Because deep caves are dark, they fill with hostile mobs. A wandering [[Creeper]] near a fresh vein can end an expedition in one blast, so experienced players light the area and wall off tunnels before digging.

## Uses

Diamonds are used to craft:

- **Tools** — pickaxe, axe, shovel, hoe, and sword, each faster and more durable than iron.
- **Armor** — a full set gives strong protection and, unlike gold, lasts a long time.
- **Stations** — the enchanting table (with diamonds and obsidian) and the jukebox both require them.

Diamond tools are also the prerequisite for upgrading to [[Netherite]], which is done at a smithing table.

## Making the most of them

Diamonds pair naturally with [[Enchanting]]: an enchanted diamond pickaxe with Fortune yields more diamonds per vein, and one with Efficiency mines dramatically faster. Careful players enchant their gear before heavy use so the durability isn't wasted on an un-enchanted tool.

See also: [[Crafting]] for how diamond gear is assembled, and [[Mining]] for reaching the ore safely.
