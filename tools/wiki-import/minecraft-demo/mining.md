# Mining

![Pixel-art pickaxe striking stone]({{media:mining.png}})

**Mining** is the act of digging through the world of [[Minecraft]] to gather stone, ores, and other resources. It is the foundation of almost everything a player builds, since nearly every tool, weapon, and machine begins with materials pulled out of the ground.

## Tools and tiers

Blocks are broken with a **pickaxe**, and the pickaxe's material determines both speed and what it can harvest. Wood and stone pickaxes handle common stone and coal; iron is required for gold, redstone, and [[Diamond]]; and diamond (or [[Netherite]]) is required for obsidian. Using too weak a pickaxe on a valuable ore destroys the block and drops nothing, so knowing the tiers matters.

The [[Enchanting]] system greatly improves mining: **Efficiency** speeds up digging, **Unbreaking** extends tool life, **Fortune** multiplies ore drops, and **Silk Touch** collects a block intact instead of its usual drop.

## Techniques

Players use several digging patterns depending on their goal:

- **Branch mining** — dig a long corridor and cut evenly spaced side tunnels to expose the most stone with the least effort.
- **Strip mining** — clear out a large area at a chosen depth.
- **Cave diving** — explore natural cave systems, which expose ores quickly but are full of hostile mobs.

## Dangers

Deep mining is risky. Lava pools sit near the lowest layers and will destroy a player and their dropped items instantly. Darkness spawns monsters, so a wandering [[Creeper]] can collapse a tunnel or a fall into an unseen cavern can be fatal. Cautious miners carry torches, blocks to wall off hazards, a water bucket, and never dig straight down into the unknown.

Once ore is gathered, it is refined and assembled through [[Crafting]], and clever players automate parts of the process with [[Redstone]].

See also: [[Diamond]] for the ore most miners are after, and [[The Nether]] for a dimension with its own dangerous mining.
