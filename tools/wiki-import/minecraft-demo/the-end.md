# The End

![Pixel-art End island with obsidian pillar]({{media:the-end.png}})

**The End** is the final dimension of [[Minecraft]], a barren archipelago of pale stone islands floating in a black void. It is home to the game's traditional boss, the Ender Dragon, and reaching and conquering it is considered the closest thing Minecraft has to "beating" the game.

## Reaching the End

Unlike [[The Nether]], the End cannot be entered through a player-built portal from scratch. Players must first find a **stronghold**, an underground fortress located using Eyes of Ender — items made from blaze powder (from a Blaze rod) and an ender pearl. Inside the stronghold is an inactive **End portal**, which is activated by placing Eyes of Ender into its frame. Stepping through transports the player to the central End island.

## The Ender Dragon

The central island is ruled by the **Ender Dragon**, a large flying boss that circles a set of tall obsidian pillars. Each pillar is topped by an **End crystal** that heals the dragon, so the fight begins by destroying the crystals — often the hardest part, as they sit high in the air. Once the crystals are gone, players damage the dragon as it swoops and perches.

Defeating the dragon drops a huge amount of experience for [[Enchanting]] and opens a gateway. It also spawns a **dragon egg**, a trophy block, and — for players who return — the dragon can be respawned with End crystals for repeat fights.

## Beyond the dragon

Killing the dragon opens portals to the **outer End islands**, reached through small gateway portals with an ender pearl. There, players find **End cities** containing valuable loot, including the elytra — wings that allow gliding flight and transform how players travel across the world. The outer islands also grow chorus fruit and shulkers, whose shells craft the portable storage boxes many players rely on.

See also: [[The Nether]] for the Blaze rods needed to reach the End, and [[Diamond]] for the gear most players bring to the dragon fight.
