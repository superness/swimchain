# Netherite

![Pixel-art netherite ingot]({{media:netherite.png}})

**Netherite** is the strongest tier of tools and armor in [[Minecraft]], sitting one step above [[Diamond]]. Netherite gear is more durable, slightly more powerful, and — crucially — survives being dropped in lava, which makes it the endgame material of choice for serious players.

## Obtaining netherite

Netherite is not mined directly. Players first gather **ancient debris**, a rare ore found only in [[The Nether]], deep among the netherrack. Ancient debris resists explosions and can only be mined with a [[Diamond]] or netherite pickaxe. Because it hides in a dimension full of open lava, many players hunt it with beds (which explode) or TNT to blast through netherrack quickly, or simply branch-mine at the right depth — see [[Mining]].

Smelting ancient debris yields **netherite scrap**. Four scrap combined with four gold ingots makes a **netherite ingot**, the material used for upgrades.

## Upgrading gear

Netherite items are made by **upgrading** existing diamond gear at a **smithing table**, using a netherite ingot and a smithing template. This means a player must already own diamond tools and armor before they can go netherite — the two tiers form a clear progression.

Upgrading keeps any [[Enchanting]] already on the item, so the usual approach is to fully enchant diamond gear first, then upgrade it. The result is armor that gives a small knockback resistance and gear that floats in lava instead of burning, so a careless step near a lava pool no longer means losing an entire kit.

## Why it matters

Because netherite survives lava and lasts so long, it is the standard loadout for the hardest content: mining ancient debris itself, exploring [[The Nether]], and facing the Ender Dragon and End cities in [[The End]]. Paired with Mending from [[Enchanting]], netherite gear can effectively last forever.

See also: [[Diamond]] for the prerequisite tier, and [[The Nether]] for where ancient debris is found.
