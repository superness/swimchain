Why this exists

A place to exist online without being the product.

Modern platforms eventually optimize for extraction: your attention sold to advertisers, your emotions farmed for engagement, your communities shaped by algorithms you can't see. Swimchain exists so that a few simple promises can actually hold — enforced by protocol design, not by a company's goodwill.

The promises

What can't happen here.

Each promise holds because the machinery it would need has been designed out — not because someone pinky-swore.

No server to seize · no company to acquire

No central authority.

No one owns the network or the data, so no one can manipulate it. The chain is replicated across every node and protocol rules apply equally to everyone.

There's no admin to pressure and no headquarters to raid — and if a community is ever captured, it forks away and leaves the attacker an empty chain.

No feed to inject into · no metrics to sell

No advertisement.

Advertising isn't forbidden — it's economically pointless. There's no placement to buy, no targeting, and no way to prove an ad was ever seen.

Posting costs proof-of-work instead of being paid for by ads, so nothing in the system needs to farm your attention or keep you scrolling.

One ranking · same water for everyone

No political influence.

There's no algorithm to buy and no amplification to capture. Everyone sees the same content, ranked the same transparent way — a shared experience, not fragments tailored to manipulate each person individually.

Propagandists can speak, but they can't force anyone to listen. Without organic engagement, their content simply decays away.

Keypair identity · nothing to warehouse

No profiling data to sell.

Your identity is a keypair — not an email, a phone number, or a real name. Every user runs a node, so there's no central server watching what you read or building a profile.

There is nothing to collect and no one to sell it to.

People · spaces · conversations

Just social media.

For participants, not consumers. Content lives while the community engages with it and drifts away when it stops mattering — attention is life support, and the community is the moderator.

The network is its users. If every developer disappeared tomorrow, the pool keeps swimming.
