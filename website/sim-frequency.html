What you're looking at

The dial is this node's concentration — how much of what it serves and reads lives in one namespace versus the rest of the network. Each frame, the page hands those weights to swimchain_core::frequency::evaluate_drift and draws the real result.

Below 90% the node stays on the base network and peers with everyone. Cross 90% and it drifts onto its space's frequency — a real value derived by hashing the namespace — and its links to base peers go dark; it keeps only peers that share that frequency. Drift it up, then back down: it doesn't rejoin until 75%. That gap is hysteresis, straight from the real constants, so a node hovering at the boundary can't flap. A deliberate subscriber that asked for that one space stays reachable the whole time — isolation is a hard wall to the crowd, not to intent.

It's the same chain and the same content throughout; only who this node discovers changes. Read on the protocol page, or see it in the wild on The Daily Drift.
